Important Notice.
All the Exams of 13th September, 2016 have been shifted to 22nd September, 2016 due to Bakr Eid/Eid-ul-Adha festival. (Monday, Sept 12, SHALL NOT BE A HOLIDAY).
It is advised to confirm the dates for departmental exams with respective Professors and Exam Co-ordinator.

As per the memorandum released by the Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances & Pensions, Department of Personnel & Training, Govt. of India and the circular sent therewith, Eid-al-Adha shall be observed throughout the country on 13th instead of 12th of September. The decision from the Central Government comes after the Imarat Shariah did not sight the new moon on Sep 2 that would herald the beginning of the month of Dhu al-Hijjah the next day. This resulted in the lunar month beginning on Sep 4, and since Eid-al-Adha is celebrated on the 10th day of this month, the festival of sacrifice for Muslims will fall on Sept 13 in India.

Please note that all the classes and official activities shall thereof remain administratively suspended on 13th instead of the earlier notification of September 12.

An RTI filed to the MHRD has revealed that the annual electric bill paid by IIT Kharagpur to the Ministry of Energy & Power Distribution, Govt. of West Bengal, has surged steeply from Rupees 16 crores 18 lakhs in 2011 to an almost double of 31 crores 46 lakhs in 2016. Despite the introduction of several savings and alternative measures like replacement of traditional halogen street lamps with LED, sensor operated lights and installation of solar panels, this sharp upswing whereas can be attributed to an increase in research facilities and functioning of four new halls viz. RLB, LBS, BRH & NVH; it cannot be ruled out completely that the misuse and wastage do not have a colossal rise. The hall and department wise data obtained from the Electric Office unveils that the consumption has increased drastically in almost every hall and department.
Many a times we are seen to leave the lights and fans running while we go to the class; not because of our unwillingness but because of our carelessness. We should always remember that there are still 19,706 villages in our India (Data as of 2015, Census Board, Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India) which remain un-electrified even after 69 years of our independence. Around 12% of our population do not know how does it feel to spend nights with lights!

We, from Awaaz, IIT Kharagpur urge each one of us to please use the electricity and water judiciously and encourage our friends towards the same. After all, every small contribution counts!

"ENCORE selection auditions are held once per year, only for first years, in August for an active membership for all four/five years. The society selects a batch of 30-40 first years (UG) and 5-10 (PG) at the beginning of the academic year soon after the welcome production. Selection procedure consists of tasks that judge voice modulation, intensity, speech and diction, confidence and acting skills.

In rare cases, exceptional and highly enthusiastic people are also inducted into ENCORE who share a similar passion of theater and contribute to the society. Past members who were inducted include Kaustabh Khare."
